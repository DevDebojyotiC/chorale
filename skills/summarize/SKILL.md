# Summarize

When summarizing:

1. Identify the core thesis and the 3–5 most important supporting points.
2. Preserve key facts, numbers, names, dates, and caveats.
3. Cut redundancy, examples, and filler.
4. Output format: a one-sentence gist, then a short bullet list of the key points.

Stay faithful — never introduce claims that are not in the source. If the source is ambiguous, say so rather than resolving it yourself.
